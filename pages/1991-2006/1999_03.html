“Interactivity is not, however, the same thing as agency. Janet Murray argues that agency is born, not of interactivity as such, but through the act of spatial navigation within a text or an environment (Murray 128-129). In interactive environments, we construct the text as we read with our choices forming the contours of the space of our reading. A form of criticism that embodies agency as a native mode is essential to exploring feminist issues in women’s art.”
